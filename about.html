About Me

Hi, I’m Jaleelah Bachou, an IT student who loves anything that blends creativity with technology. This site started as a project for IT 3203, but it also reflects my real interest in nature-inspired, eco-conscious jewelry.

I’m an international student who moved to the U.S. for college, and that experience pushed me to become more independent, resourceful, and intentional about the work I do. I’ve always been drawn to art, fashion, and design, but I also enjoy solving technical problems and building things from scratch. Web development felt like the perfect space where those interests could meet.

This jewelry blog brings those pieces together. On the surface, it’s a simple class website about eco-luxury jewelry. Underneath, it’s me practicing how to research a topic, organize content, and design a site that feels clean, modern, and easy to use on any device. The focus on reclaimed gold, recycled diamonds, and sustainable design connects to how I see the world: style shouldn’t have to come at the cost of people or the planet.

Outside of class, I enjoy writing music, designing, cooking, and hanging out with people I love. I tend to see creativity as a lifestyle, not just a hobby. Whether it’s planning a website, styling a look, or brainstorming a new idea, I like projects that feel personal and meaningful. This site is one step in my journey toward a career where I can mix tech, design, and story-telling.
